Balancing Wisdom: Unveiling My Diverse Horizons in Extra Curricular Activities.

I believe in embracing a well-rounded lifestyle. In addition to my love for coding and technology, I actively participate in various extracurricular activities that not only contribute to my personal growth but also allow me to give back to the community. One of my favorite pursuits is teaching tech to aspiring juniors, sharing my knowledge and experience to inspire the next generation of developers. Additionally, I engage with various tech-related organizations that provide a platform for networking, learning, and collaborating with like-minded individuals.

. Hosting Hands-On Practical Workshops.

During my junior year of study, I had the wonderful opportunity to organize and conduct several enriching workshops, one of which was a highly successful Web Development workshop tailored specifically for sophomores. Over three intensive days, I guided students in creating a fully functional Banking web application. The workshop covered a comprehensive curriculum, starting from fundamentals and building knowledge step by step.

Throughout the workshop, my focus was on imparting essential skills, teaching participants how to code in HTML, CSS, and utilize Bootstrap for responsive web design . Additionally, I introduced them to the world of databases by teaching SQL from scratch, enabling seamless data storage and retrieval. To make their web applications dynamic and interactive, I shared my expertise in hosting web servers and creating robust classical backends using PHP.

The resounding success of this workshop led to its high demand. In my senior year, I conducted the Web Development workshop once again, continuing the tradition of empowering and nurturing the next generation of web developers.

I also orchestrated a transformative Computer Integration workshop Free of cost as a way to give back to the community. During the workshop, I guided the participants through building personalized computers from scratch and provided essential information on requirement specifications, hardware selection, compatibility issues, and cost reduction. As students assembled PCs, I offered concise explanations on the significance of each computer part, resulting in an empowering experience where participants gained practical skills and a deeper understanding of the foundation on which our softwares live.

At the end of the workshop, I further demonstrated the associated relevance of this knowledge by delving into Virtual Machine Orchestration. I provided a glimpse of Docker and Kubernetes, showcasing how these powerful tools enable Software Containerization. By understanding the building blocks of computers and the intricacies of VM Orchestration, participants were equipped with valuable insights into both hardware and software aspects of modern computing.

. Coordinator - Computer Society of Xaviers (CSX).

The Computer Society of Xaviers (CSX) stands as a prestigious institution, empowering students with transformative experiences in technology. Through captivating guest lectures, workshops, project exhibitions, competitions, and call for paper publications , CSX fosters an environment of innovation and knowledge exchange.

In 2018, I was humbled to be elected as the Head Coordinator, by the unanimous votes of department faculties and a personal recommendation of the dean , overseeing the participation of over 720 college students in a dynamic array of activities. CSX embodies the relentless pursuit of excellence, shaping the future of technology and education, and inspiring the next generation of tech pioneers to push the boundaries of possibility.

. The Voice of the Class: My Role as Class Representative.

During my undergraduate, I had the incredible honor of serving as the class representative for four consecutive semesters in my junior and senior years, a position that spoke to the trust and confidence my fellow students and esteemed faculty had in me. I took immense pride in being consistently elected, advocating for my peers' needs and concerns, acting as the bridge between faculty and students, and fostering a strong sense of community and collaboration.

Organizing class-related events brought us together like a tight-knit family. This experience revealed my innate ability to lead, inspire, and make a positive impact on others, values that I carry as a software developer, driving my commitment to excellence in every project.
